# Common Objections & Responses

## Price Objections

### "You're more expensive than Triple Whale/Lifetimely"

**Response:**
> "We're not comparing apples to apples. Triple Whale is a self-serve software tool at $129/month. We're a done-for-you service that includes:
> - Custom data pipeline built for your business
> - White-glove setup and training
> - Ongoing maintenance and optimization
> - Monthly strategy calls
> - Custom development when you need it
>
> Compare our $3,000/month to hiring a data analyst at $80,000/year + benefits. That's our real competition—not a dashboard you set up yourself."

**Key point:** Reframe from "software cost" to "service value."

---

### "The setup fee is too high"

**Response:**
> "The setup fee covers 20-30 hours of custom development—building your data pipeline, designing your dashboard, integrating all your platforms, and training your team. 
>
> If you hired a data consultant to do this, it would cost $10,000+. If you tried to do it in-house, it would take weeks and probably not work right the first time.
>
> We get you live in 1-2 days with everything working. That's what the setup fee buys you."

**Alternative for tight budgets:**
> "We can amortize the setup fee over the first 3 months if cash flow is tight. Let's talk about what works for your business."

---

### "We're already paying for [other tool]"

**Response:**
> "That's not a conflict—it's a complement. Most of our clients use a tool like Triple Whale for day-to-day media buying and Rior for profit intelligence and strategic decisions.
> 
> Here's the difference: [Other tool] shows you revenue and ROAS. We show you net profit and break-even ROAS per SKU. One is for buying ads, the other is for knowing if you're actually making money.
>
> If [other tool] is already answering all your questions, great. But if you still can't tell me your true net profit last month—or which SKUs are actually profitable—then you need what we provide."

## Product Objections

### "Can I see a demo of the actual dashboard?"

**Response:**
> "Absolutely. We have a live demo at /demo with sample data. But more importantly, your dashboard will be *custom built for you*—not a template.
>
> During setup, we interview you about your business, your current reporting gaps, and your decision-making process. Then we build a dashboard that answers *your* specific questions."

**Action:** Always push toward the live demo.

---

### "What if the numbers don't match Shopify?"

**Response:**
> "They won't match exactly—and that's usually good. Here's why:
> 
> Shopify shows gross revenue. We show net profit. To get there, we subtract:
> - COGS (your actual landed costs, not just a percentage)
> - Shipping costs
> - Payment processing fees
> - Returns and refunds
> - Attributed ad spend
>
> If you want a full walkthrough of how we calculate every number, we do that during setup. You'll see exactly how we arrive at net profit."

---

### "Do you have an attribution model?"

**Response:**
> "Yes—we support first-click, last-click, linear, and data-driven attribution. But here's the thing: attribution models all have flaws. 
>
> Our focus is on *profit intelligence*, not just attribution. Even if the attribution isn't perfect, if you know your contribution margin and break-even ROAS per SKU, you can make profitable decisions.
>
> That said, if you have a preferred attribution model or want to compare models, we can build that for you."

---

### "How is this different from just using Google Analytics?"

**Response:**
> "Google Analytics is great for website behavior, but it has major gaps for e-commerce profit analysis:
> 
> 1. **No cost data** - GA doesn't know your COGS, shipping costs, or payment fees
> 2. **Limited ad platform integration** - GA struggles with iOS 14+ attribution
> 3. **No profit calculations** - It can't tell you if a sale was actually profitable
> 4. **No SKU-level break-even** - Can't calculate break-even ROAS per product
>
> We pull from your actual Shopify orders, your actual ad spend APIs, and your actual cost data. That's how we calculate true net profit."

## Service Objections

### "What happens if you go out of business?"

**Response:**
> "Great question—and this is actually a major advantage of working with us. Unlike SaaS tools where your data lives in their system, with Rior:
>
> 1. **Your data is in YOUR BigQuery instance** - You own it completely
> 2. **Your dashboards are in Looker Studio** - You keep access even if we part ways
> 3. **Your pipelines are documented** - Any developer can pick them up
>
> If we ever part ways, you keep everything. You just stop getting updates and support. Compare that to canceling Triple Whale—your data disappears."

## Competitive Objections

### "We're evaluating Northbeam/Daasity too"

**Response for Northbeam:**
> "Northbeam is excellent for attribution. If you need the most sophisticated multi-touch attribution model and have a team of media buyers optimizing daily, they're a good choice.
>
> But if you want to know your *actual profit*—not just which ad drove the click—Northbeam doesn't calculate that. They focus on attribution, we focus on profit intelligence.
>
> Also, Northbeam is typically $999+/month with annual contracts. We're usually half that with month-to-month flexibility."

> "Daasity is a solid choice if you have an internal data team and want to build everything yourself. They give you the data warehouse and raw data access.

>
> Also, Daasity typically starts at $5,000/month with a 4-8 week setup. We're faster and less expensive."
