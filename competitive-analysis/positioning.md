# Rior Systems Competitive Positioning

## Positioning Framework

**Rior's Core Position:** Done-for-you Profit Intelligence for Shopify brands spending $20k-$200k/month on ads.

**Key Differentiators:**
1. True net profit calculation (not just revenue attribution)
2. White-glove setup and ongoing support
3. You own your data in BigQuery
4. Custom development included

---

## Competitor-by-Competitor Positioning

### 1. Triple Whale

**Their Position:** All-in-one e-commerce OS for DTC brands

**Their Strengths:**
- Easy self-serve setup
- Strong brand/community
- First-party pixel
- Good attribution modeling
- Lower price point

**Their Weaknesses:**
- No true profit calculation (revenue-focused)
- No SKU-level break-even ROAS
- No white-glove onboarding
- Limited customization
- You don't own your data

**Rior's Position vs. Triple Whale:**
> "Triple Whale is great if you want a self-serve tool for attribution. But if you're spending $50k+/month on ads and need to know your *actual* net profit per SKU—including COGS, shipping, and payment fees—you need more than a dashboard. You need someone to build it for you, customize it to your business, and make sure it's right. That's Rior."

**When to win:**
- Prospect has tried Triple Whale but still can't see true profitability
- Prospect needs SKU-level break-even calculations
- Prospect wants custom metrics/alerts specific to their business
- Prospect is frustrated with chat-only support

---

### 2. Northbeam

**Their Position:** Enterprise-grade attribution for sophisticated marketers

**Their Strengths:**
- Best-in-class attribution modeling
- First-party data with Apex
- Real-time data
- Advanced features (MMM, creative analytics)
- Strong for large spenders ($500k+/mo)

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Expensive ($999+/mo for full features)
- Complex setup (2-4 weeks)
- No profit calculation—purely attribution
- Heavy focus on media buyers, not CFOs
- Enterprise contracts, hard to cancel

**Rior's Position vs. Northbeam:**
> "Northbeam is the gold standard for attribution if you're spending $500k+/month and have a team of media buyers. But if you want to know whether you're actually *profitable*—not just which ad drove the click—you need profit intelligence. We calculate break-even ROAS per SKU, factor in all your costs, and give you a dashboard your CFO will actually trust. Plus we're 1/3 the cost."

**When to win:**
- Prospect is considering Northbeam but sticker-shocked
- Prospect needs profit visibility, not just attribution
- Prospect wants faster setup (days vs. weeks)
- Prospect wants month-to-month flexibility

---

### 3. Lifetimely (now Amp)

**Their Position:** LTV and profit analytics for Shopify stores

**Their Strengths:**
- Good profit calculations
- LTV cohort analysis
- Affordable pricing ($149-$499/mo)
- Fast setup
- AI-powered insights (new)

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Self-serve only—no custom development
- Limited customization
- Basic alerts
- No data warehouse
- Chat/email support only

**Rior's Position vs. Lifetimely:**
> "Lifetimely is solid if you're comfortable setting up and managing your own analytics. But most founders at your stage don't have time to become data analysts. We build everything custom for you—from data pipeline to dashboards to alerts. If something's wrong, we fix it. If you need a custom metric, we build it. It's like having a data team for less than an entry-level hire."

**When to win:**
- Prospect has outgrown Lifetimely's self-serve model
- Prospect needs custom metrics/alerts
- Prospect wants strategic guidance, not just a tool
- Prospect is spending enough to justify white-glove service

---

### 4. Peel Insights

**Their Position:** Automated business intelligence for Shopify brands

**Their Strengths:**
- Good Shopify integration
- Automated reporting
- Some profit visibility
- White-glove onboarding

**Their Weaknesses:**
- No transparent pricing (custom/enterprise)
- Limited ad platform integrations
- No real-time data
- Less flexible than Daasity, less guided than Rior

**Rior's Position vs. Peel:**
> "Peel gives you automated reports, which is nice. But they don't give you a data warehouse you own, and their ad platform integrations are limited. We connect everything—Shopify, Meta, Google, TikTok—and put it all in your own BigQuery instance. You get deeper insights, full data ownership, and ongoing custom development."

**When to win:**
- Prospect wants transparent pricing
- Prospect needs TikTok/ full ad platform coverage
- Prospect wants to own their data
- Prospect needs faster data refresh

---

### 5. Daasity

**Their Position:** Data warehouse + analytics for mid-market e-commerce

**Their Strengths:**
- Full data warehouse included
- Raw data access
- Custom development available
- Good for larger operations
- You own your data

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Expensive (typically $5k+/mo)
- Long setup (4-8 weeks)
- Heavy DIY component—you need analysts
- No profit-specific features out of the box
- Annual contracts

**Rior's Position vs. Daasity:**
> "Daasity is great if you have a data team and want to build everything yourself. But if you want a working profit dashboard in 1-2 weeks—not 2 months—and you don't want to hire analysts to maintain it, that's where we come in. We give you the same data warehouse foundation, but we build the profit logic, alerts, and dashboards for you. Plus we're typically half the cost."

**When to win:**
- Prospect was quoted Daasity but wants faster time-to-value
- Prospect doesn't have internal data resources
- Prospect wants profit-specific insights, not just raw data
- Prospect wants month-to-month vs. annual contract

## Summary Battle Cards

| If they say... | You say... |
|----------------|------------|
| "Why should I pay more?" | "You're not paying for software. You're paying for someone to build, maintain, and optimize your profit system. Compare us to hiring a data analyst at $80k/year." |

| If they say... | You say... |

### General Positioning Statements

**For attribution-focused prospects:**
> "Attribution tells you which ad drove the click. Profit intelligence tells you if that click made you money. We do both, but profit is what keeps the lights on."

**For overwhelmed founders:**
> "You shouldn't need to become a data analyst to understand if your business is profitable. We build it, we maintain it, we explain it. You just make decisions."

**For CFOs/finance:**
> "All your data in one place. Fully auditable. Calculated with your actual costs—not platform estimates. That's what we deliver."

**For operators/CMOs:**
> "Know your break-even ROAS for every SKU. Get alerted when to scale and when to cut. That's the difference between profit and burning cash."
